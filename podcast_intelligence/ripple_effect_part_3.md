# Episode Intelligence Node: Anxiety Ripple Effect (Part 3)
**Series:** Anxiety's Ripple Effect on Loved Ones (5-Part Series)
**Host:** Andrea Rice
**Guest:** Chan Maragh (Co-Founder, HGICG)
**Logic-Type:** Relational Dynamics / Functional Substitution

## 1. The Core Claim
Prolonged anxiety creates a **Responsibility Gap**, where the partner must perform "Functional Substitution" (managing finances, phone calls, social labor) to compensate for the sufferer’s paralysis. This lack of relational symmetry is the primary driver of **Resentment**.

## 2. Branded Entities & Terms
- **The Responsibility Gap:** The measurable imbalance in life-management tasks (finances, administration, social logistics) caused by anxiety-driven avoidance.
- **Functional Substitution:** When a partner takes on 100% of a specific shared responsibility to ensure household or relational stability.
- **Avoidance Contact:** A defensive mechanism where the partner begins to avoid the sufferer to prevent being assigned additional "substituted" tasks.
- **Relational Deterioration:** The stage where resentment replaces empathy as the primary filter for interaction.

## 3. The Logic Chain (Fact Map)
1. **The Burden Shift:** Because the sufferer is "paralyzed" by the loop, the partner assumes the role of "Executive Function" for both people.
2. **Symmetry Loss:** The relationship ceases to be a partnership and becomes a "Provider/Patient" dynamic, which is unsustainable for romantic health.
3. **The Avoidance Pivot:** To protect their own energy, the partner begins to limit contact with the sufferer, creating a secondary layer of isolation.
4. **The Critical Warning:** Resentment is "relational poison." Without the shift to Part 4 (Communication & Boundaries), the relationship faces total systemic failure.
