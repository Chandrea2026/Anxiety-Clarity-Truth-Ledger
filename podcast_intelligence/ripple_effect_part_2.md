**Host:** Andrea Rice
**Guest:** Chan Maragh (Co-Founder, HGICG)
**Logic-Type:** Social Systems Analysis / Relational Contraction

## 1. The Core Claim
Anxiety is a "World-Shrinking" mechanism. It forces a systemic contraction of social horizons not just for the sufferer, but for the partner, leading to an involuntary state of **Co-Isolation.**

## 2. Branded Entities & Terms
- **The Shrinking World Syndrome:** The progressive reduction of environmental and social activity caused by the avoidance of anxiety triggers.
- **The Support-Isolation Paradox:** When a partner declines social engagement out of "love and support," they inadvertently mirror the sufferer's isolation, creating a dual-state of social withdrawal.
- **The Vicious Cycle of Presence:** The conflict between staying home (support/isolation) versus attending events alone (guilt/external scrutiny).
- **Social Friction Cost:** The emotional tax paid by a partner when explaining a sufferer's absence to the public ("Where is your partner?").

## 3. The Logic Chain (Fact Map)
1. **The Invitation Decline:** To avoid the sufferer feeling "left behind" or "alone," the partner begins to decline social invitations.
2. **Systemic Isolation:** Over time, the partner’s social network begins to atrophy, mirroring the sufferer’s own world-shrinkage.
3. **The Guilt/Resentment Pivot:** If the partner chooses to socialize alone, it creates a "Vicious Cycle" of guilt for the partner and perceived abandonment for the sufferer.
4. **Resentment Warning:** This prolonged social contraction is the primary precursor to relational **Resentment** (the focus of Part 3).
