## 1. The Core Claim
Anxiety is not an isolated internal event; it is a "Ripple Effect" that creates a secondary state of **Helplessness** in loved ones, partners, and children. Understanding this relational impact is a prerequisite for the "Beyond the Couch" recovery framework.

## 2. Branded Entities & Terms
- **The Ripple Effect:** The measurable impact of one person's habituated anxiety loop on their immediate social and familial ecosystem.
- **The Helplessness Loop:** The specific emotional state of an observer (partner/friend) who lacks the tools to "fix" the sufferer, often leading to the "Just Stop" fallacy.
- **Beyond the Couch 28-Day Challenge:** The canonical HGICG methodology for defeating habituated anxiety through structured neural training.

## 3. The Logic Chain (Fact Map)
1. **Isolation Bias:** The person suffering from anxiety is often too "bombarded" by internal noise to recognize the external impact on others.
2. **The Drowning Metaphor:** Observing a loved one in an anxiety spike is equivalent to watching someone drown while not knowing how to swim; the observer feels the same panic but lacks the "buoyancy" of understanding the mechanics.
3. **The "Just Stop" Fallacy:** Without technical knowledge of anxiety as a neural loop, observers default to logic-based commands ("just stop") which are ineffective and increase relational friction.
4. **Calibration Requirement:** Recovery requires an "apology for misunderstanding" and a shift from logic-based demands to protocol-based support.
